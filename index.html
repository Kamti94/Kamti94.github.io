Achievements

JP Morgan - Code for Good, 2016

Dream a Dream is a company that uses their Tribrid-Bioreactor biogas plant to reuse industrial waste and generate methane rich biogas that can in turn be used to power a Cogen engine that generates electric power. We worked over a period of 36 hours to come up with modifications to their existing Tribrid-Bioreactor design and propose a control system that can automate and optimize the efficiency of their bioreactor to run at a 100 percent efficiency irrespective of the type, concentration and amount of substrate (waste) that was dumped into the bioreactor.

My contribution to the project was to come up with a 2 staged neural network architecture that first models the biogas plant as a state machine, by training a Recurrent Neural Network to emulate the reactor. This Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) is then used to emulate the biogas plant and a simple Multilayered Neural Newtork is trained to control the output of the RNN (the state variables) to drive the system from some random state to the desired state, by minimizing a specifically designed error function.
